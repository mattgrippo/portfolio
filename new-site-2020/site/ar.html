Landing Pages

Audience: Operations Associates

Problem:

Needed a way to reach operations with high priority content that is separate from the general Elearnings

Results:

Developed landing page that gathered 10,000 total page visits and 500 unique visitors in 3 months following launch

Description:

I was tasked with creating multiple Elearning courses for different technologies and processes that AR develops. We realized that a centralized location for the content would be beneficial, so I developed a landing page that would host information that was specific to an audience or topic. Below the content is divided by type, including: documentation, one-pagers, videos, and Elearnings.

Course Development

Audience: Operations Associates and Technical Engineers

Problem:

Needed a way to rapidly produce courses in Articulate Storyline for multiple audiences and be able to manage and maintain these courses efficiently

Results:

Designed and created a Storyline template which supported the creation of 30 courses

Total Course completions over 5 months: 6,852

Description:

My team requested courses to be developed that would emphasize the user experience, rather than a replication of the Powerpoints that our SMEs were handing off to us. This process involved thinking about the specific audience, and tailoring the delivery of the content in a way that was more easily understood. One of the solutions was to divide the content into digestible “chunks”, to help users isolate concepts in a comprehensible way that would allow for larger relationships to become clear.

One pagers

Audience: Operations Associates

Problem:

Needed a way to physically reach our audience that was high visibility

Results:

Developed a style and template for one pagers and then used this template to create 5 one pagers for our audience

Description:

As Prime Day came and went, we realized we needed a way to reach associates off their screens, quickly and efficiently. We had important messages that needed to be communicated to them regarding best practices, that needed to be top of mind during a shift. We decided to create one pagers that would allow for employees to see the information physically, hanging up on the wall across from their work station. This created new conditions for us to consider, like making the text legible from a certain distance away.
