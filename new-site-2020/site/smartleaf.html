Design Requirements

When I first started my role at Smartleaf, I was assigned to manage the digital ads and analytics of our website through Google services.

I was creating ads for a cohesive digital experience but the website I was sending our users to was poorly designed and did not compliment my work. Their graphics needed a refresh and the lack of interactivity on the site made information gathering static and dull. I felt this was important to their brand, as the company offers cutting edge financial software and their digital presence should match this level of technological service. So I approached management with the idea to redesign their website.

I worked with the executive team to identify places for improvement that would benefit the overall image of the firm. My goals were to incorporate a flat design that was simple and easy to navigate, to match the current trends of the industry.

New Solutions Page

Some of the things we decided to focus on:

  • Refreshing the overall look and feel
  • Improving the interactivity and storytelling
  • Providing a better user experience to support the sales funnel


Design Results

I focused on better incorprating the colors of the brand to help users navigate the site, including creating cohesive call-to-action buttons across all web pages. I also provided more opportunities for our audience to interact with the site, in an effort to reduce our exit percentage.

I was able to create a flat design that made the site easy to digest, which I achieved through solid blocks of color with minimal borders and lots of white space.

I also focused on improving the home page by adding icons and giving the audience the ability to watch demos that feature specific aspects of our product.
