Dawsome was an interaction design class project that I designed over the course of a semester. The process involved every stage in the design process including researching, interviewing, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, and branding. Define

The Define stage involves a lot of interviewing and researching to determine:

  • The audience that you solving for
  • The problem that they are experiencing

After interviewing a variety of people and having them talk about their daily frustrations, I settled on a particular persona, amatuer bands. Being apart of a band myself, I was aware of many of the problems and frustrations that amatuer bands could face. So I decided I wanted to create an online Band Manangement System to help amatuer bands keep track of things like practice times, song writing, and gig opportunities.

Once I decided on a project, I continued to conduct interviews to determine what features and capabilities would be the most valuable to my audience. In my interviews, I realized that there was another problem that amatuer bands were facing and my solution was not addressing it, the recording process. I found that even though a Band Management System would be helpful, the audience already had solutions to the organizational and communicative problems they faced (a mix of Google Drive and Facebook Messanger in most cases).

This is when I decided to pivot my idea into an online Digital Audio Workstation (or DAW for short) to enable collaboration between musicians, particularly band members, whether they are in the same room or across the world. And so I continued to interview appropriate subjects to determine what my audience was truly looking for in a recording solution such as this.

Design

I now had data from my interviews about the frustrations local musicians face during the recording process and features that they would find most useful, so I started to come up with concepts for the platform.

I wanted the site to let users be able to comment on a song at a certain point in time. I benchmarked services like Soundcloud for their inteaction and social features, and then I looked at programs like Garageband to understand audio editting user interfaces. DAWs require a lot of functionality to be useful but somehow I still wanted to simmer down what the essense of a digital audio workstation was.

Some considerations that went into my design:

  • Allowing multiple users to work and edit on a song
  • The ability to upload and export audio on the site
  • A simple organization for the editting process (recording, mixing, and editting)

Test

The prototype created from my designs was centered around three tasks that I wanted the audience to use my tool for. These tasks were:

  • Uploading a song to the DAW
  • Editting the song in the DAW
  • Sharing the song with other accounts

The test provided a lot of insight into my prototype. The signifier showing that the Effects (Fx) options were on a seperate tab was not obvious enough. I also learned that a lot of the audio editting icons I was using were not recognizable to my audience. While they were inspired by existing icons in DAWs, the audience for my site was amatuer bands who may not have this background knowledge.

Brand

In the gallery is a series of concept ideas for a Dawsome logo.

As the design came together, I decided to use an icon that symbolizes the loop function in most DAWs. The logo represents the collaboration of musicians and the cycle of work that becomes a song.
